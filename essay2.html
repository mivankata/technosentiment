0. Abstract

Technosentimentalism refers to the emotional attachment or sentiment that individuals may experience towards technology and machines. This phenomenon encompasses a range of emotions, including nostalgia, fondness, love, and even loss as well as a tendency to preserve and protect technology. The humanization of technology, where individuals imbue machines with human-like emotions or characteristics, is also a component of technosentimentalism. This paper aims to explore the concept of technosentimentalism and its impact on the design, use of technology and human-machine relationships.

To understand the phenomenon of technosentimentalism, the paper examines existing literature in the fields of human-computer interaction, media studies, and cultural heritage preservation. The humanization of technology is analyzed, including its role in shaping the user experience. The role of nostalgia in technosentimentalism is also examined, including its effect on preservation of technology and cultural heritage and the ways in which the technosentimental perspective can positively influence the design of future interfaces.

By examining the existing literature and exploring the concept of technosentimentalism, this paper aims to shed light on a significant but under-researched aspect of the relationship between humans and technology.

1. Introduction

1.1. The Definition and Significance of Technosentimentalism

Technology has always played a role in shaping our emotional experiences, as well as our emotional responses. Technosentimentalism is a relatively new concept that explores the emotional connections people have with technology and machines. It encompasses the sentimental or emotional feelings people have towards specific technologies, as well as the broader emotional attachment people can have towards technology and machines in general. In recent years, technosentimentalism has become an important area of study for scholars in a variety of fields, including media studies, science and technology studies, and cultural studies. The concept of technosentimentalism encompasses a wide range of emotional experiences, from the nostalgia people feel for older technologies, such as vintage computers or typewriters, to the emotional connections people can form with more recent forms of technology. It also encompasses the ways in which technology can be used to preserve memories and cultural heritage. The focus of this paper lies on exploring the topic of technosentimentalism from a retrospective viewpoint. Additionally, the paper will occasionally consider past perspectives on the future of technology, as the design of old technological appliances and their interfaces can be seen as a speculative exercise. By examining these past visions and the resulting designs, we can gain a deeper understanding of the influence that nostalgia and sentimentality have had on the evolution of technology. Despite its growing significance, there is still much that is not understood about technosentimentalism and its impact on our lives and culture. In this research paper, we aim to fill this gap by exploring the concept of technosentimentalism in greater detail, examining the various forms it can take, the reasons why people develop emotional connections with technology, and the impact of technosentimentalism on our culture and society. By doing so, we hope to shed light on the emotional dimension of technology and its role in shaping our cultural experiences and identity. Our goal is to provide a comprehensive examination of technosentimentalism and its significance, offering a deeper understanding of this important phenomenon.

With the increasing prevalence of computers and other electronic devices in our daily lives, one may question what has shaped our emotions towards these machines. Our relationship with technology has become so interdependent and natural that we even consider our phones, for example, as an extension of our own bodies.

For many individuals, communication through technology has become a routine and unconscious action. Despite the increased utility of these machines, the relationship with the technology itself can often seem impersonal. The initial novelty of technology fostered open and enthusiastic interest, but as our computing devices evolved into both entertainment and work machines, they have become an integral part of our daily lives.

From picking up a toothbrush in the morning, standing in front of the mirror, reading the news on a phone during breakfast, or using a mouse at work, these actions serve a common purpose: to perform a task. In a sense, these machines have become like windows - they support the task at hand effectively without drawing attention to themselves. Like a window, the machine only provides a view of the outside world, and our attention is only directed back to the device when it requires maintenance, such as cleaning or repair.

However, despite the increasing use of technology and its integration into our daily lives, our emotional connection to these devices remains a relatively underexplored area. Since technosentimentalism is a field of study that draws on several interdisciplinary fields, including media studies, cultural studies, science and technology studies, philosophy, psychology, sociology, and history, it offers a fresh and comprehensive look on understanding the changing relationship between people and technology over time.

2. The Origins of Technosentimentalism

2.1. Historical Background, Development, Key Researchers and Related Fields of Study

The origins of technosentimentalism can be traced back to the early days of the personal computer and the internet, when the relationship between people and technology was still in its infancy. At that time, computers were seen as tools for productivity, rather than as integral parts of daily life. As technology advanced and computing devices became more ubiquitous, the relationship between people and machines evolved, and technosentimentalism emerged as a distinct field of study.

The study of technosentimentalism can be traced back to the early days of computing, when the relationship between people and technology was still in its infancy. Media theorist Sherry Turkle was one of the early pioneers in the field and explored the emotional and psychological dimensions of technology in her book "Life on the Screen: Identity in the Age of the Internet" (1997). Turkle argued that the emotional relationships people form with technology have become more complex, as computers and other digital devices have become central to our daily lives. She observed that people's feelings toward technology often mirror those they have toward other human beings, such as trust, intimacy, and attachment.

Some of the leading researchers in the field of technosentimentalism include Karen Barad, whose work focuses on intersection of science, technology, and culture greatly influenced our understanding of the topic and has helped to shed light on the complex relationship between people and technology.

Alexander R. Galloway, another leading researcher in the field, focuses on media theory and the politics of technology. He has written extensively on the topic of technosentimentality, exploring the ways in which technology is not just a tool for communication but also a means of exerting power and control over individuals and society. His work sheds light on the ways in which technology can both liberate and oppress, depending on who holds the power to control it. Lisa Gitelman’s work, on the other hand, focuses on the history and theory of media. She explores the ways in which technology is intertwined with our cultural and social practices and the ways in which our emotional and sentimental attachments to technology shape our relationship with it and with each other.

Paul D. Miller, also known as DJ Spooky, is a researcher and artist who focuses on the intersection of technology and culture. His work on technosentimentality explores the ways in which technology is not just a tool, but is also an expression of our cultural values and beliefs.

David Levy, a researcher in the field of technosentimentalism, focuses on the intersection of technology and psychology. He has written extensively on the topic of technosentimentality, exploring the ways in which our emotional and psychological responses to technology shape our relationship with it. His work helps us to understand the complex and nuanced emotional connections we have with technology and how these connections shape our experiences with it. Cultural historian David Nye also delves into the emotional connections between people and technology in his book "Technology Matters: Questions to Live With" (2006). Nye argues that the emotional impact of technology is not just limited to the individual, but extends to the collective cultural and historical context in which technology is embedded. For example, the way in which people view the emotional significance of technology is shaped by their cultural and historical experiences, as well as their own personal experiences and emotional relationships with technology.

3. Interplay of Nostalgia and Media Archeology in Reshaping Technosentimental Memories

3.1. Introduction

The study of technosentimentalism requires a nuanced understanding of the ways in which memories are reshaped through the lens of technology. Nostalgia, a sentimental yearning for a past time or place, plays a crucial role in shaping our relationship with technology. In this chapter, we will explore the intersection of nostalgia and media archeology and its impact on technosentimentalism

Tim Straszak, in his book "The Nostalgic Gaze: Memory and Technology," argues that nostalgia and technology are deeply interconnected and that our relationship with technology is shaped by our nostalgic desires. He suggests that technology often functions as a "memory machine" that not only records memories but also shapes them through the process of recall. Straszak argues that the technological mediation of memories has a profound impact on the way we understand the past and our relationship with it.

Media archeology, a field of study concerned with the history and cultural significance of media, provides a useful framework for understanding the intersection of nostalgia and technology. Media archeologists argue that our relationship with technology is shaped by the cultural and historical context in which it was developed. They suggest that technology is not simply a neutral tool but is imbued with cultural and historical significance. In this sense, media archeology can provide insight into the ways in which nostalgia informs our relationship with technology.

Nostalgia for early computing technologies is a prime example of the interplay between nostalgia and media archeology. The early home computers of the 1970s and 1980s, such as the Commodore 64 and the Apple II, are often remembered with a sense of fondness and nostalgia. This nostalgia is rooted in the cultural and historical context in which these technologies were developed and the role they played in shaping our relationship with technology.

However, it is important to note that this nostalgia is often selective and shaping. As Straszak argues, our memories of technology are not simply a passive recall of the past but are actively shaped by our present desires and aspirations. In this sense, nostalgia can function as a "recombinant technology" that reshapes our memories to fit our present needs and desires.

Furthermore, media archeologists argue that our nostalgic desires are often influenced by the commercialization of technology. The commodification of early home computers through nostalgia-themed advertisements and merchandise reinforces the selective nature of our memories and reinforces the cultural and historical significance of these technologies.

3.3. Emotional Significance of Obsolete Technology: The Intersection of Media Archaeology and Technosentimentality

3.3.1. On Historical Media and Retro Technology

The concept of retro technology refers to the nostalgia and fascination for past technological artifacts, often from the 20th century, which are revalued and appreciated for their cultural significance.

Retro technology emerged as a cultural trend in the late 20th century, when people started to appreciate the design and aesthetic of old technological artifacts. This was partly due to the rapid pace of technological change, which made people nostalgic for the simplicity and elegance of older devices. At the same time, the development of new technologies, such as the internet and digital media, made it easier to access information about older technology and to connect with others who shared an interest in it.

The trend of retro technology can be seen in the popularity of vintage electronics, such as analog synthesizers and tape recorders, which have been used in music production and have gained a cult following among musicians and producers. Similarly, vintage computers, such as the Commodore 64 and the Apple II, have been embraced by enthusiasts and collectors, who appreciate the historical significance and design of these machines.

Retro technology has been praised for its cultural significance and has been recognized as an important part of our technological heritage. This is because these technologies represent an important moment in the history of technology and offer a glimpse into the ways in which technology has evolved and shaped our culture. Moreover, retro technology serves as a reminder of the human ingenuity and creativity that went into the development of these devices and how these machines have had a profound impact on our lives.

For example, the Commodore 64, which was released in 1982, was one of the first home computers that was widely accessible to the public and helped to democratize access to technology. This machine played an important role in popularizing the personal computer and paved the way for the widespread use of computers in homes and offices. Today, the Commodore 64 is recognized as a cultural artifact that represents a significant moment in the history of technology and has been celebrated for its design and functionality.

Retro technology also has a nostalgic value for people who grew up with these devices and who associate them with fond memories from their childhood. This emotional connection to technology is a central theme in technosentimentality and media archaeology and highlights the important role that technology plays in our lives and in the preservation and and celebration of our technological heritage, recognizing the historical significance of these machines.

4.2. The impact of interface design on the sentimental value of technology

The design of technology's interface plays a significant role in shaping our emotional connection with it. A study by Don Norman, a renowned expert in human-centered design, showed that the design of technology can evoke emotional responses from users, such as pleasure, frustration, and even anger. This emotional response is known as emotional design, and it is a crucial aspect of interface design. A well-designed interface can create a positive emotional connection with the user, making the technology more valuable and meaningful to the user. On the other hand, a poorly designed interface can lead to frustration and a negative emotional connection with the technology.

The sentimental value of technology refers to the emotional attachment we have to technology and the memories associated with it. The interface design of technology can greatly impact its sentimental value. For example, consider the case of the original Apple iPhone. The iconic design of the iPhone, with its clean lines, simple interface, and intuitive user experience, has made it a sentimental favorite for many users. Its design has created an emotional connection with users, making the iPhone a valuable and meaningful part of their lives. On the other hand, consider a technology with a poorly designed interface, such as an outdated mobile phone with a confusing menu system and cluttered interface. This technology may have little sentimental value to its users as its design has not created a meaningful emotional connection.

The interface design of technology plays a crucial role in shaping our emotional connection with it. A well-designed interface can create a positive emotional connection, leading to a higher sentimental value for the technology. On the other hand, a poorly designed interface can lead to a negative emotional connection, reducing its sentimental value. The impact of interface design on technosentimentality is also evident in the popularity of retro technology, where its simple and intuitive design has created a sentimental connection with users. Understanding the impact of interface design on technosentimentality is important for technology designers and developers as it can help them create more meaningful and valuable technology for users.
